❤️ OUR STORY

Created by Stressed Students, for Stressed Students

The untold story of how we stopped highlight-coding and started coding active recall.

The Yellow Highlighter Disaster

In 11th grade, we did what every standard NEET aspirant does. We went to a big coaching institute, bought massive bundles of thick modules, and bought a pack of neon yellow highlighters. By October, our NCERT books didn't look like books anymore—they looked like radioactive yellow glow-sticks.

We read the same chapter on Plant Kingdom 15 times. But every time the Sunday test came around, we still mixed up the examples of green, brown, and red algae. The problem wasn't that we weren't studying. The problem was that **passive reading is a scam**.

Stumbling on Spaced Repetition

One night, while procrastinating on YouTube instead of solving physical chemistry numericals, we stumbled upon the science of **active recall** and **spaced repetition**. The scientific papers said that the best way to move data into long-term memory is to force your brain to recall it right before it's about to forget it.

We tried using standard medical student flashcard apps (like Anki), but they were too complicated to set up and didn't have specific line-by-line questions for Indian NEET prep. So, we decided to build our own app. We spent late nights coding, fetching questions, and parsing NCERT pages.

The Birth of MEDNEET

MEDNEET was born out of frustration. We designed it to be exactly what we needed during our own high school days:

  • A dark-mode aesthetic that doesn't burn your eyes during midnight sessions.
  • Direct NCERT line-by-line checks so you don't waste time on college-level concepts that are outside the syllabus.
  • Daily streaks to make consistent study feel like playing a video game.
  • An analytics heatmap so you can easily spot your weaknesses before the actual coaching test series exposes them.

We hope this app saves you from the backlog anxiety and helps you secure your dream government medical college seat. Remember, study smart, eat some almonds, and stay hydrated!
